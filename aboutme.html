About me

Indraneel Mukherjee: From Sales Strategist to Web Developer & Digital Marketer

I’m Indraneel Mukherjee, a passionate and purpose-driven professional based in Gaya, Bihar. With a B.Tech in Electrical and Electronics Engineering and over five years of experience in the EdTech and media sales industry, my journey has been anything but linear — and proudly so. I’ve worked with renowned organizations like Byju’s, UpGrad, The Times Group, and Torry Harris Business Solutions (THBS). I’ve experienced the grind of cold calling, the excitement of closing big-ticket deals, and the satisfaction of leading ad campaigns.

However, I believe true growth often comes from introspection and reinvention. After a successful sales career, I chose to pause and reflect. I took a career break to prepare for the Bihar Public Service Commission (BPSC) — a path driven by the desire to make a meaningful difference in public life. Though I eventually shifted directions, that period became a foundation for my self-discipline, civic awareness, and personal growth.

Today, I am embracing a new and exciting chapter — transitioning into the world of web development and digital marketing, blending technical skills, creative strategy, and my core strength in communication to build impactful digital solutions.

Professional Experience: Sales with Strategy and Heart

My professional foundation was laid in the EdTech and media industries, where I held key sales roles at some of India’s top companies:

Byju’s (2017–2018)

As a Senior Sales Executive, I managed end-to-end B2C and B2B sales. I conducted educational seminars, visited institutes, and helped launch partnership programs. I gained exposure to Below-the-Line (BTL) marketing, lead acquisition strategies, and hands-on training with sales teams — all while exceeding monthly targets consistently.

UpGrad (2018–2019)

Here, I expanded on my EdTech experience as a Senior Sales Advisor. I was responsible for course sales, creating sales pitches tailored to lead funnel stages, training new joiners, and deploying email marketing for lead acquisition. My work also included pipeline management and cold calling to filter qualified leads.

Times Group (2019–2021)

My role as a Senior Sales Executive involved similar responsibilities but with a sharper focus on cross-selling, lead nurturing, and email-based customer outreach. I also began experimenting with sales content writing, creating dynamic scripts for different types of leads, laying the foundation for my interest in content-driven marketing.

Torry Harris Business Solutions (THBS) – Sales Manager (2021–2022)

This was a pivotal role that involved more than just sales. I managed B2B and B2C operations, led vendor onboarding, and collaborated with educators to build courses that aligned with audience needs. I also became actively involved in digital marketing — creating ad copies and creatives, running campaigns on Meta and LinkedIn, and contributing to strategic brand positioning and value propositions.

Through all these roles, I developed a keen understanding of people, persuasion, performance, and how data meets decision-making in business environments.

The Career Break: A Pause with Purpose

In 2022, I made the difficult but fulfilling choice to step away from my corporate trajectory to prepare for the BPSC exams. My motivation was rooted in public service, and I spent the next year immersed in intensive preparation.

Preparing for BPSC was a serious commitment. I spent over a year focused on topics like polity, history, geography, and public administration. It was intense, self-driven, and mentally demanding. While I didn’t end up pursuing a career in civil services, the process reshaped my mindset.

It taught me the value of discipline, clarity of thought, and a deeper understanding of governance and social systems. Most importantly, it gave me the courage to restart, learn again, and create a new path for myself with complete honesty and dedication.

Discovering the World of Web Development and Digital Marketing

As I re-evaluated my future, I asked myself three simple questions:

  • What excites me enough to keep learning every day?
  • Where can I combine creativity with strategy?
  • What industry will always evolve and grow?

The answer was clear: the digital ecosystem.

I began by teaching myself the basics of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Initially, I just wanted to understand how websites are built. But soon, I found myself deep-diving into concepts like responsive design, DOM manipulation, and frontend frameworks like AngularJS. I'm currently also exploring Node.js to strengthen my backend skills.

Simultaneously, I leveraged my sales and advertising experience to explore digital marketing — not just from a theoretical perspective, but from a creator’s point of view. I studied SEO (on-page and off-page), social media marketing, paid ad campaigns on Meta and LinkedIn, and email marketing workflows.

I started working on my own projects — from writing ad copies and designing creatives on Canva, to running demo campaigns and testing landing pages. I began crafting a new professional identity — one where I’m not just selling solutions, but building them.

From Sales Expertise to Full-Stack Thinking

My previous experience in sales gave me a deep understanding of customer psychology, communication, and strategic outreach. These skills translated naturally into digital marketing — crafting persuasive copy, segmenting audiences, building funnels, and analyzing campaign metrics.

More importantly, the switch to development and marketing has allowed me to approach problems from both the user and builder perspective. I don’t just think about how to drive traffic to a page — I also think about how to design and code that page for optimal performance and experience.

This dual skillset — part coder, part marketer, and part communicator — is what I’m building into my personal brand.

Skills I’ve Gained and Applied

  1. Frontend Web Development: HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, responsive design, DOM manipulation.
  2. Frameworks & Tools: AngularJS, Node.js, WordPress (Elementor), Git
  3. Digital Marketing: SEO, Meta Ads, LinkedIn Ads, Instagram strategy, email campaigns

  5. Soft Skills: Communication, team collaboration, problem-solving, people management

Why This Shift Makes Sense

Though the transition from sales to software and marketing may seem drastic from the outside, it makes complete sense to me. Every skill I built over the years — be it pitching a product, crafting a value proposition, or managing a team — is still relevant. What’s changed is the medium.

I’m no longer just a facilitator between a product and a customer. I’m becoming the creator of those products — both visually and functionally.

This shift has not only been career-oriented but deeply personal. I’m doing work that excites me, challenges me, and aligns with how I want to contribute in the long term — through digital problem-solving, design thinking, and human-centric tech.

What I’m Looking For

I’m now actively looking to join a team, internship, or freelance collaboration where I can contribute as a web developer, digital marketer, or creative strategist. My ideal work environment is one that values curiosity, experimentation, and clear communication.

I’m especially drawn to startups, creative agencies, and mission-driven businesses that need hybrid talents — someone who can code a landing page and optimize it for conversions, someone who understands both the product and the pitch.

If given the opportunity, I bring with me:

  • A real hunger to learn and grow
  • A unique blend of sales, marketing, and development skills
  • A mindset shaped by resilience, self-study, and reinvention
  • And above all, an honest desire to add value wherever I go

Final Words

My journey — from selling courses and managing ad campaigns, to preparing for a public service career, and now building web apps and digital strategies — is not typical, and I don’t want it to be.

I believe in second chances, in learning from scratch, and in the power of self-directed growth. My story isn’t just about changing careers — it’s about owning your story and being brave enough to rewrite it.

So whether it’s a campaign to design, a website to build, or a story to tell — I’m here to make it impactful, authentic, and user-first.

Let’s build something meaningful together.
